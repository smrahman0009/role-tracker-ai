# Demo prep checklist

> **Reference doc, snapshot.** A pre-demo checklist for recording a
> portfolio video. Use as a guide; the live app may have evolved beyond
> the specific screens described here. For current operational details,
> see [`HANDBOOK.md`](HANDBOOK.md).

Before recording a demo for LinkedIn / a portfolio / a recruiter
showcase, run through this list. The goal is a clean, professional
recording that doesn't expose anything you'd rather keep private OR
anything that could read as a public statement about other companies.

## Before recording

- [ ] **Empty the "Hidden companies" list** — Settings → Hidden
      companies → "Clear all". Or scroll past this section in the demo.
      Showing specific company names on a "hidden" list, even framed as
      personal preference, can come across as confrontational and may
      cost you opportunities with those companies later.
- [ ] **Empty the "Hidden title keywords" list** — same reasoning.
- [ ] **Empty the "Hidden publishers" list** — Settings → Hidden
      publishers → "Clear all". Listing specific job-board publishers
      as filtered carries the small but non-zero risk of being read as
      a public assessment of those services.
- [ ] **Use only your own data in Saved Searches** — the queries you
      actually run (Data Scientist, ML Engineer, etc.) are fine; they're
      role descriptions, not company names.
- [ ] **Verify your Contact Info** — make sure the live preview shows
      what you want and nothing you don't (toggle off any field you
      don't want public).
- [ ] **Generate ONE letter for the demo, then mark unrelated jobs as
      applied** so the list is concise and on-message. Long lists of
      jobs you didn't generate letters for look unfinished.
- [ ] **Open the Job Detail page on a strong example** — pick a letter
      that scored well (≥ 90/110) so the critique panel reads as a
      proof point, not a weakness.

## During recording

- [ ] **Start on Job List**, not Login. The login screen is just a
      placeholder; the value is in the matched-jobs view.
- [ ] **Tour: list → click into a job → letter → strategy panel →
      refine textarea → download.** That's the core story.
- [ ] **Mention prompt caching, the rubric scoring, and the strategy
      phase out loud.** These are the technical differentiators most
      AI app demos don't have. Recruiters and engineers notice.
- [ ] **Skip Settings briefly OR scroll past the Hidden lists.** Show
      Resume, Contact Info, and Saved Searches. Skip the bottom three
      sections.
- [ ] **Don't read filter values aloud** if the camera catches them.
      Especially salary minimums or specific company names you've added.

## After recording

- [ ] **Re-populate your filters** if you cleared them for the demo —
      they're useful for actual use.
- [ ] **Watch the recording on mute, scrubbing through every second.**
      Look for: any visible API key in the URL bar, any embedded
      Hidden-list values, any company names in tooltips or popovers.
      It's easy to miss things on the first watch.
- [ ] **Crop or blur** anything sensitive before publishing. Most
      screen-recording tools have built-in blur.

## Phrasing for the LinkedIn post / video voiceover

Frame everything as **personal AI assistant** rather than **job-board
gatekeeper**:

✅ "I built an AI assistant that helps me filter and apply to jobs."
✅ "It generates a tailored cover letter using a self-critique loop
    against a rubric I designed."
✅ "Settings let me filter sources and roles I'm not targeting."

❌ "It blocks scammy job boards." (judgmental about specific entities)
❌ "I've excluded these companies." (named publicly = bad signal)
❌ "These publishers are untrustworthy." (defamation-adjacent)

## If you accidentally publish something problematic

- The legal risk for a personal-filter UI showing publisher / company
  names is **low but non-zero**. Defamation requires a published
  factual claim that's false and harmful. A personal filter is closer
  to "I prefer X" than "X is bad," but the framing matters.
- If you get a complaint, most can be resolved by deleting the post
  and apologising. Real lawsuits over a single LinkedIn post are
  rare and would require demonstrable damages.
- The bigger risk is reputation — recruiters at named companies
  quietly closing doors. That's harder to undo.

When in doubt: **don't show the Hidden lists in public content.** Keep
them private to your local instance.
